The 6 Key Pollutants

We monitor and analyze six critical air pollutants to provide you with the most accurate Air Quality Index (AQI) predictions and health advisories.

PM2.5

Fine Particulate Matter

Tiny particles (≤2.5 micrometers) from vehicle exhaust, power plants, and fires. Because they are so small, they can bypass the nose and throat and penetrate deep into the lungs and blood.

PM10

Coarse Particulate Matter

Inhalable particles (≤10 micrometers) found near roadways and dusty industries. They can irritate the eyes, nose, and throat, and aggravate asthma.

NO₂

Nitrogen Dioxide

A highly reactive gas primarily emitted by vehicle engines and power plants. High levels inflame airways, worsening coughs, wheezing, and breathing difficulty.

SO₂

Sulfur Dioxide

Produced predominantly from the burning of fossil fuels (coal and oil) by power plants. Short-term exposures can trigger breathing problems, especially in vulnerable groups.

CO

Carbon Monoxide

An odorless, colorless gas generated by incomplete burning of fuels, most notably in cars and trucks. It reduces the amount of oxygen that can be transported in the blood.

O₃

Ground-Level Ozone

Not emitted directly into the air, but created by chemical reactions between oxides of nitrogen and volatile organic compounds (VOC) in the presence of sunlight.
